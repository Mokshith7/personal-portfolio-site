I was recently trekking near the Nanda Devi biosphere. We were summiting a peak called Pangarchulla. What I love about these treks is that, they take your mind off from mundane things like slack, outlook, standups etc., There is no network and hence doom scrolling phone isn't an option.

This gives a lot of room for introspection and some genuine conversations with people. I had the privilege to meet with one such person called Veer. He was a fit young man at his late 50s. Yes, I am intentionally using the word "young" because the quality of youth has nothing to do with age. We had some really profound conversations. Here is one thing I took away from one of our conversations. That Rome was not built in a day.

I remember us talking during the trek and I was complementing his fitness. I was complaining that since I worked with an IT Firm, my days are not structured and hence it is difficult to plan my workouts and this is what he said:

> "Mokshith, my days are unstructured too. But I know I need to run everyday. There are times when I ran midnight, there are times when I ran early morning. Do not focus too much on the bigger goal. Just worry about today. Did you work towards your goal today? That's all that matters."

This might not sound like an eye opener, but I am a person who always looks at the peak even before the journey begins. But this conversation made me realise that while the peaks are important, what is more important is just taking a step towards that peak, then one more step and then one more, and just like that without even looking at the peak, without even stressing about the peak, you would have reached the peak.

I have always wanted to write. But I wanted to write big and grand, but now, I realise that if I just write everyday, without focusing on the "grander writing" I might reach there. Rome was not built in a day, but the romans laid bricks everyday, one after the other quietly and just like that Rome was built.
