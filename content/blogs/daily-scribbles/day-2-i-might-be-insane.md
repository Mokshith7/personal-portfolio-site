I am back with yet another story from my recent trek. Please bear with me! This was our last day trekking, we were descending from Khullara campsite to Tugasi. Tugasi is a very picturesque small village in Chamoli. The people over there were very warm. Kids were happily playing around and hunting for chocolates from travellers, unaware of anything that has been going on with the world. Fluffy little dogs were our guides there. They expected nothing but a few bites of biscuits from us.

We stopped at a small house for maggi and when we were eating, a beautiful lady probably in her 40s asked us to make way as she was carrying something. I noticed how ornate, and beautiful her jewellery was (probably some pahadi design?). But what was more beautiful was her broad smile. She had a very innocent and genuine smile on her face and that smile never went away even after we met her after sometime when we descended.

Our trek guide told us later that she is insane (he didn't say the word, but just made a gesture). This pushed me into a spiral of thoughts, hear me out. Obviously a sane person wouldn't smile that genuinely at strangers, because we are taught not to. As sane people we are taught to be less expressive, less approving, more defensive. In that moment, I felt a little happy for that woman. She is happy because all the "sane" teachings might have never reached her. And we should be a little insane too!

If sanity is what makes us build walls, then I am an insanely vulnerable person.

If sanity is what makes us laugh quieter, then I am an insane howler.

If sanity is what makes us embarrassed for who we are, then I am insanely me.

Be insane my friend! Be insane!
