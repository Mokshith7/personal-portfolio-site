Today marks the beginning of my painting journey. I've always admired art from afar, but something pushed me to finally try it myself.

## Setting Up

I started with a basic watercolor set - 12 colors, a few brushes, and some watercolor paper. Nothing fancy, just enough to get started.

## First Attempts

My first attempt was a simple gradient wash. I learned quickly that watercolors have a mind of their own. The water spreads in unexpected ways, creating beautiful accidents.

## What I Learned

- Patience is key - you have to let layers dry before adding more
- Less water means more control, but sometimes the unexpected is more beautiful
- There's no such thing as a mistake, just happy accidents as Bob Ross would say

Looking forward to tomorrow's practice!
